# Aetna (commercial)

> **SIMULATED policy reference.** Derived from patterns in public Aetna
> clinical-policy bulletins (CPBs). Not authoritative.

Aetna's national clinical-policy bulletins (e.g., CPB 0228 for cardiac
catheterization, CPB 0086 for stress testing) drive the policy posture.
The CPBs are detailed and procedure-specific, and Aetna applies them
fairly uniformly across product lines.

## Prior-authorization posture

Aetna requires PA for invasive cardiology (LHC, EP study) and applies
medical-necessity criteria to non-invasive imaging (echo, nuclear
stress) without a hard PA requirement. The plan publishes appropriate-
use criteria that mirror ACC/AHA guidelines.

## Procedure-by-procedure

### 93015 — Treadmill stress
PA not required. Aetna's CPB on stress testing emphasizes indication
matching to appropriate-use criteria; mismatches trigger
post-payment review.

### 93306 — TTE
PA not required. Frequency criterion: not more than one TTE per
6-month interval for stable conditions absent an interval clinical
change.

### 93458 — LHC with contrast
**PA required.** Aetna's CPB requires either (a) high-risk findings on
non-invasive testing or (b) a clinical scenario meeting class I/IIa
appropriate-use criteria. Documentation: full non-invasive study
report (not just summary), symptom history, risk factors.
Turnaround: 5–7 business days.

### 93620 — EP study
**PA required.** Aetna's CPB demands documented arrhythmia + symptoms
+ failure or contraindication of pharmacologic therapy. Turnaround:
5–10 business days.

### 93224 — Holter
PA not required. Aetna's CPB caps monitoring duration; longer
recordings (≥7 days) require separate PA under different codes
(93241–93248).
