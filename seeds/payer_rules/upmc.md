# UPMC Health Plan

> **SIMULATED policy reference.** Derived from patterns in public UPMC
> Health Plan medical-policy documents. Not authoritative.

UPMC Health Plan is the other dominant commercial payer in Western
Pennsylvania, and frequently the secondary or out-of-network counterpart
to Highmark. The plan operates an integrated delivery-payer model with
UPMC hospitals, so site-of-service rules are stricter than Highmark's
for procedures that COULD be performed at a non-UPMC facility.

## Prior-authorization posture

UPMC Health Plan applies prior authorization to most invasive cardiology
procedures and to nuclear-imaging stress tests. Office-based studies
(stress treadmill, echo, Holter) are usually allowed without PA but are
subject to retrospective review.

A relevant nuance for referring practices: UPMC HP frequently auto-routes
PA approvals when the requesting provider is in the UPMC network,
shortening the turnaround. Out-of-network providers see longer queues
and may need to attach a network-adequacy waiver.

## Procedure-by-procedure

### 93015 — Treadmill stress
PA not required for in-network providers; out-of-network may face PA.
Retrospective review on documentation of indication.

### 93306 — TTE
PA not required; retrospective review may apply if billed at hospital
outpatient site of service vs. office.

### 93458 — LHC with contrast
**PA required.** Document: prior non-invasive testing, anginal
symptoms (Canadian Cardiovascular Society class), and absence of an
absolute contraindication. UPMC HP turnaround averages 2–4 business
days for in-network providers, longer for out-of-network.

### 93620 — EP study
**PA required.** Documentation of the suspected arrhythmia mechanism
is the key driver — non-specific symptoms alone do not pass policy.
Turnaround: 4–6 business days.

### 93224 — Holter
PA not required. Site-of-service rule: prefer office over hospital
outpatient when clinically equivalent.
