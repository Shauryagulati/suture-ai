# Highmark Blue Cross Blue Shield (Pennsylvania)

> **SIMULATED policy reference.** Derived from patterns in public Highmark
> medical-policy bulletins and prior-authorization lists. Not authoritative
> — for the real, current policy a clinician must consult Highmark's
> provider portal at the time of order entry.

Highmark BCBS PA is the dominant commercial payer in Western Pennsylvania.
Two product families to be aware of: commercial PPO (typically the loosest
prior-auth requirements) and the Highmark Health Options Medicaid/CHIP
plans (tightest). The rules below reflect the COMMERCIAL PPO posture.

## Prior-authorization posture

Highmark commercial generally distinguishes between:
- **Non-invasive office-based cardiology** (treadmill stress, TTE, Holter)
  — usually allowed with diagnosis-supported medical necessity, no PA.
- **Advanced imaging or invasive procedures** (nuclear stress, LHC, EP
  study) — PA required, with required-document checklist.

The medical-policy line that catches most denials is the distinction between
93015 (cardiovascular stress test, treadmill — physician-supervised) and the
related-but-separate 93017/93018 codes for nuclear imaging components.
Code carefully: **93015 alone usually does not require PA**; 93015 + 93017
(nuclear stress combined billing) usually does.

## Procedure-by-procedure

### 93015 — Cardiovascular stress test, treadmill
PA not typically required for the bare treadmill code. Submit the order
with the diagnosis code and the patient's relevant cardiac history.
Denials cluster around: missing functional-capacity rationale, recent
identical study within 60 days.

### 93306 — Transthoracic echocardiogram (TTE)
PA not typically required. Plan-side review may flag back-to-back echo
within 90 days without an interval clinical change. For HF management,
documenting the EF measurement intent is helpful.

### 93458 — Left heart catheterization with contrast
**PA required.** Highmark expects documentation of: a prior non-invasive
study or its medical-necessity bypass, anginal symptom characterization
(CCS class is helpful), and risk-stratification rationale. Typical
turnaround on the auth queue: 3–5 business days.

### 93620 — EP study (comprehensive)
**PA required.** Documentation: arrhythmia documentation (Holter, event
monitor, telemetry strip, or ECG), prior pharmacologic trial result,
patient functional status / symptom impact. Turnaround: 5–7 business days.

### 93224 — 48-hour Holter monitor
PA generally not required, but site-of-service review may apply for
hospital-outpatient billing vs. office. Common denial: documentation
gap on duration / symptom correlation.
