# UnitedHealthcare (UHC) commercial

> **SIMULATED policy reference.** Derived from patterns in public UHC
> commercial medical-necessity policies and advance-notification lists.
> Not authoritative.

UnitedHealthcare combines hard prior-authorization rules with an
"advance notification" (notify-only) layer for certain procedures.
For cardiology, advance-imaging codes are the most common touchpoint.
Behavior is also driven by the UHC subsidiary handling the case
(UHC commercial vs. UHC West vs. Optum subsidiaries).

## Prior-authorization posture

UHC delegates many cardiology PA decisions to a clinical-review vendor
(Optum). The provider experience is therefore a portal handoff, not a
direct UHC review. Required-document checklists are checklist-driven.

## Procedure-by-procedure

### 93015 — Treadmill stress
PA not required. Advance notification may be requested for high-
volume practices under specific contracting.

### 93306 — TTE
PA not required. UHC sometimes flags repeat TTE within 60 days
without documentation of interval change.

### 93458 — LHC with contrast
**PA required.** UHC requires: prior non-invasive testing OR
documented acute coronary syndrome, ECG, troponin trend (if ACS),
and clinical rationale. Turnaround through Optum: 3–5 business days,
sometimes faster on weekday morning submissions.

### 93620 — EP study
**PA required.** Documented arrhythmia + failed medical management
or contraindication to medical management. Turnaround: 4–7 business
days.

### 93224 — Holter
PA not required for 48-hour recording. Longer monitoring (mobile
cardiac telemetry, MCT) under separate codes requires PA.
